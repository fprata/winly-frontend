If you've ever searched for public tenders in Europe, you've encountered CPV codes. They're the backbone of how EU procurement is organized — and getting them right is essential for finding relevant opportunities.

## What Are CPV Codes?

CPV stands for **Common Procurement Vocabulary**. It's a standardized classification system used across all EU member states to describe the subject of public contracts.

Every CPV code is an **8-digit number** followed by a check digit:

```
72212517-6
│││││││  │
││││││└──┤ Division (2 digits)
│││││└───┤ Group (3 digits)
││││└────┤ Class (4 digits)
│││└─────┤ Category (5 digits)
│└───────┤ Subcategory (6-8 digits)
└────────┘ Check digit
```

## The CPV Hierarchy

The system is hierarchical. Higher-level codes are broader:

| Level | Example | Description |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Division | **72** | IT services |
| Group | **722** | Software-related services |
| Class | **7221** | Systems and software consulting |
| Category | **72212** | Programming services |
| Subcategory | **72212517** | IT development software |

## How to Find Your CPV Codes

### Step 1: Start Broad

Think about what your company sells. Is it a product (supplies), a service, or construction work?

- **Divisions 01-44** → Supplies (goods)
- **Divisions 45** → Construction works
- **Divisions 50-98** → Services

### Step 2: Narrow Down

Use the official EU CPV search tool or explore the hierarchy:

**Common divisions for SMEs:**
- **48** — Software packages
- **50** — Repair and maintenance
- **71** — Architectural, engineering, and planning
- **72** — IT and related services
- **73** — R&D services
- **79** — Business services (consulting, legal, marketing)
- **80** — Education and training

### Step 3: Select Multiple Codes

Most companies match **3-10 CPV codes**. Don't limit yourself to one — buyers may classify similar needs under different codes.

For example, a web development agency might use:
- 72212000 — Programming services of packaged software
- 72413000 — Website design services
- 72212517 — IT development software
- 48000000 — Software packages and information systems

## Why CPV Codes Matter

### 1. They Determine What You See

When you search for tenders on TED or national portals, CPV codes are the primary filter. Wrong codes = missed opportunities.

### 2. Buyers Use Them to Find Suppliers

Many procurement authorities search supplier databases by CPV code. Being registered with the right codes increases your visibility.

### 3. They Affect Competition Levels

Some CPV codes are highly competitive (e.g., general IT services), while niche subcategories have fewer bidders. Targeting specific subcategories can improve your win rate.

## Common CPV Code Mistakes

1. **Using only top-level codes** — too broad, you'll get irrelevant results
2. **Ignoring supplementary codes** — tenders can have primary and supplementary CPV codes
3. **Not updating codes** — the CPV vocabulary is periodically revised
4. **Missing cross-category opportunities** — a consulting project might appear under 79 (business services) or 72 (IT services)

## Tips for Maximizing Coverage

- **Check competitor tenders** — look at what CPV codes are used in contracts won by companies similar to yours
- **Review past tenders** — search TED for keywords related to your services and note which CPV codes appear
- **Use both primary and supplementary codes** — some portals allow filtering by supplementary CPV codes too
- **Set up alerts for multiple codes** — cast a wider net, then filter manually

## CPV Codes and AI Matching

Modern procurement platforms go beyond simple CPV matching. They analyze your full company profile — services, past experience, geographic reach, team size — and match you against tenders using AI scoring algorithms.

This means you're not limited to exact CPV matches. AI can identify relevant tenders even when the buyer has used a different classification than expected.

The key is still to define your CPV codes accurately as a starting point, then let intelligent matching expand your coverage.
